Personal growth

One of my first assignments in the course i 1Talent was making a DISC test. A DISC test determines your personal profile. The possible results of the test are the four DISC behavioural styles dominant, influential, stable and conscientious.
Making this test was relevant because it allowed me to find out what kind of person I am within a team. I am an influence and a dominant type, which turned out to be the case after the test. On the basis of the results, teams were then formed, wich resulted in groups of different personalities. A situation that occurs frequently in daily life and certainly on the shop floor. Here are some of the works I have done individually or in groups.

My DISC personality typing

My most obvious behavioral style is probably Influence, followed by Dominant. When you have Influence and After that dominant as the most important behavioural style, the positive impression you make on people: You are socially minded. You have an intrinsic motivation to make people of very different backgrounds and to maintain these relationships well. You have a natural enthusiasm for all kinds of ideas and developments, both for those of yourself as well as others. You are likely to find people social, convincing and optimistic.
